Pollution

Scroll for more.

The South Island is a Controlled Area for the invasive freshwater algae Didymo (also known as rock snot), which was first detected there in 2004. So far, no didymo has been found in the North Island.

It is a legal requirement to check and clean your gear after being in the water to prevent the spread of didymo and other waterborne pests.

Under the Biosecurity Act (1993) there is a requirement not to spread invasive freshwater weeds and pests anywhere in New Zealand.
